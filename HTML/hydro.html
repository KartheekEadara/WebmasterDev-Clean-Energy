Hydropower

What really is Hydropower?


Hydropower is a renewable energy source that generates electricity by using the energy of flowing or falling water. It works by capturing the kinetic energy of water as it moves downstream, typically through the use of dams or turbines. These turbines convert the water's energy into mechanical energy, which is then used to generate electricity. Hydropower is a clean and reliable source of energy, providing a sustainable alternative to fossil fuels while also helping to reduce greenhouse gas emissions.











What can I use Hydropower for?



  • Electricity Generation: Hydropower plants convert the energy of flowing water into electricity, which can be used to power homes, businesses, and industries.

  • Irrigation: Hydropower can be used to operate pumps that deliver water for irrigation, allowing farmers to water their crops efficiently.

  • Water Supply: Hydropower systems can pump and distribute water to communities for drinking, cooking, and sanitation purposes.

  • Flood Control: Hydropower dams can help control flooding by regulating the flow of water in rivers and streams, especially during periods of heavy rainfall or snowmelt.

  • Recreation: Hydropower reservoirs and dams can create recreational opportunities such as boating, fishing, swimming, and wildlife habitat preservation.











    • What next?


  • Research: Learn about hydropower providers in your area and the availability of hydropower options for residential use.

  • Contact Providers: Reach out to hydropower companies or utilities that offer renewable energy plans, specifically hydropower, and inquire about their services.

  • Assess Costs: Compare the costs of hydropower plans with your current electricity expenses to ensure affordability.

  • Sign Up: Once you've found a suitable hydropower provider, sign up for their renewable energy plan to start receiving 100% hydropower-generated electricity.

  • Notify Current Provider: If necessary, inform your current electricity provider of your decision to switch to hydropower and proceed with any necessary account changes or cancellations.

  • Explore Incentives: Research any available incentives or rebates for using renewable energy, such as tax credits or utility incentives, to maximize savings.

  • Spread the Word: Share your experience with friends and family to encourage others to consider switching to renewable energy sources like hydropower for their electricity needs.











    • How much is It?


    Transitioning to 100% hydropower for your electricity needs involves assessing the cost of hydropower plans offered by providers in your area. While these rates may be comparable to traditional electricity costs, leveraging tax incentives and rebates can help offset upfront expenses. In the United States, for instance, the Federal Residential Renewable Energy Tax Credit provides a credit of up to 26% of the total system cost for renewable energy installations, including hydropower. Additionally, some states offer their own incentives, such as rebates or tax credits, further reducing the cost of transitioning to hydropower. By exploring these incentives and comparing hydropower rates, you can make an informed decision about switching to clean and renewable hydropower while potentially saving money in the long term.
